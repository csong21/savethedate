  • The Backstory

    Fall 1979

    On September 1st, 1979, the Department of Physics of Nanjing University welcomed 164 students. Of those there were only 21 girls. Two of them - Ren Cheng and Yu (Jade) Tong, soon became inseparable.

    After graduation, the two best friends parted on different journeys. Ren became a college professor in Xi’an, while Jade traveled 11,563 miles away to the U.S., pursuing graduate studies. At a time where international phone calls were expensive, they continued sharing their lives through pen.

    In the same university in Xi’an where Ren taught, she met Hao Song. In the same university in U.S. where Jade studied, she met John Morton. In 1990, Juer was born. In 1992, Jamie was born.

  • First Impression

    Spring, 1995

    When Jamie was still a toddler, he was taken back to China for a short visit to Jades’s hometown. Ren, of course, took Juer to see her. The moment when Juer and Jamie first met was a total blur to both of them. Jamie couldn’t speak then, and Juer speaking no more than a few English words did not help either. Rumors were though, Jamie gave Juer a big hug.

  • Second Impression

    summer, 2007

    Flash forward fourteen years later, the two “childhood friends” met again in 2007, when Jamie traveled to China and Juer hosted him in Xi’an. Luckily, this time Jamie had learned to speak, so they talked on and on. But little did Juer know, Jamie had a deep crush on her.

  • Keep in Touch

    2008 to 2015

    They kept in touch throughout the years, as friends. Juer always saw Jamie was a dear family friend, and attributed all his extra-friendliness to their family connections.

  • Send, and Send

    Dec 30, 2018

    Ten years later, Jamie, finishing his PhD. in San Diego, finally had the courage. He sent Juer an email, confessing his feelings. Juer, living a life in New York City, freaked out. She turned to her then colleague-slash-deskmate-turned-to-friend Yfran. Over beer, she confessed that she had the same feelings for Jamie, but there were too many emotions that it was impossible to unpack in an email response. Juer asked Yfran to respond for her. Yfran saw the opportunity. He grabbed Juer’s phone, simply typed, Yes I’m interested, and clicked on the Send button.

    The rest is history.
