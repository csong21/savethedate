A long, long time ago...

  • The Backstory

    Fall 1979

    On September 1st, 1979, the Department of Physics of Nanjing University welcomed six girls. The six girls all lived in the same dorm, and as the only six girls in a class of fifty people, they were dubbed as the six flowers. Two of them - Ren Cheng and Yu Tong, soon became inseparable. After graduation, the two best friends parted on different journeys. Ren moved to Xi’an to teach at a college, while Yu traveled 7,500 miles away to U.S., pursuing graduate studies. At a time where international phone calls were expensive, they wrote to each other and kept on sharing life through pen. In the same university in Xi’an where Ren taught, she met Hao Song. In the same university in U.S. where Yu studied, she met John Morton. In 1990, Juer was born. In 1992, Jamie was born.

  • First Impression

    June 18, 2007

    In 1993, when Jamie was still a toddler, he was taken back to China for a short visit to Yu’s hometown. Ren, of course, took Juer to see her. The moment when Juer and Jamie first met was a total blur to both of them. Jamie couldn’t speak then, and Juer speaking no more than a few English words did not help either. Rumors were though, Jamie gave Juer a big hug.

  • Keeping in touch

    2013

    Flash forward fourteen years later, the two “childhood friends” met again in 2007, when Jamie traveled to China and Juer hosted him in Xi’an. Luckily, this time Jamie had learned to speak, so they talked on and on. But little did Juer know, Jamie had a deep crush on her.

  • Second Chance

    June 30, 2017

    They kept in touch throughout the years, as friends. Juer always saw Jamie was a dear family friend, and attributed all his extra-friendliness to their family connections.

  • We're Engaged

    Dec 30, 2018

    Ten year later, Jamie, finishing his PhD. in San Diego, finally had the courage. He sent Juer an email, confessing his feelings. Juer, living a life in New York City, freaked out. She turned to her then colleague-slash-deskmate-turned-to-friend Yfran. Over beer, she confessed that she had the same feeling for Jamie but had to crush it because there were so many things in play: long-distance, family, and she was not sure if Jamie really knows her because she was always trying to behave when she was with Jamie. There were so many emotions that it was impossible to unpack in the email response. Juer asked Yfran to respond for her. Yfran saw the opportunity. He grabbed Juer’s phone, simply typed, Yes I’m interested, and clicked on the Send button.
